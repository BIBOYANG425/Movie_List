## Design Context

### Users
Social-first Gen Z users who want to share taste, compare rankings with friends, and discover media through social proof. They use Spool in short, frequent sessions — ranking a new movie they just watched, scrolling the feed, checking what friends rated. Context: mobile-first, often sharing screenshots or links.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, curated, premium.** Like a collector's shelf — statement-making, taste-forward, aspirational. Your rankings say something about you. Spool should feel like a place where having opinions matters and good taste is celebrated.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Dark, warm, editorial. Gold and steel blue on deep navy. Serif display type (Cormorant Garamond) for gravitas, clean sans (Source Sans 3) for readability.
- **References**: Spotify Wrapped / Apple Music — celebrating personal taste, colorful, shareable, data-as-identity. Are.na / Cosmos — curation aesthetic, minimal, intentional, taste as self-expression.
- **Anti-references**: IMDb / Rotten Tomatoes (database-first, no personality), Generic SaaS dashboards (cards/metrics/sidebar), TikTok / Instagram (scroll-feed dopamine), Letterboxd (too community-wiki, not aspirational enough).
- **Theme**: Dark only. `#0F1419` background, warm gold `#D4C5B0`, steel blue accent `#8BA8BA`.

### Design Principles
1. **Taste is identity** — Every screen should make the user's taste feel important and worth sharing. Rankings aren't data, they're self-expression.
2. **Curated over comprehensive** — Show less, make it count. No information dumps. Progressive disclosure. Quality of presentation over quantity of features.
3. **Premium but not pretentious** — Aspirational without being exclusionary. Bold choices, warm tone, accessible language.
4. **Social through curation, not feeds** — Social features should feel like comparing collections, not scrolling timelines. Intentional sharing over broadcast.
5. **Every screen is shareable** — If a user screenshotted this view, would it look good? Would it make someone curious about Spool?
